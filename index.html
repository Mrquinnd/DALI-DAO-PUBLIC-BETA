WORK

The advent of blockchain technology presents decentralisation as an opportunity to break up monopolies and transfer ownership of the internet back to users indefinitely. The impact of this movement has affected virtually all industries, but what about fashion? In fact, creative collectives of individuals across the world are rapidly proving the power of decentralised autonomous organisations through self-determined economies governed by the users and executed through smart-contracts. In effect, this is all in pursuit of fundamentally changing the relationship between brand and consumer while empowering this generations ‘consumer-creators’ to build their own brands (Hayward, 2021) as a viable alternative to the traditionally wasteful and globally corrosive industry .

WHY DAO?

A DAO is one of the first examples of an organisational structure that has the beneficial characteristics of the firm (minimising transaction costs, aggregating capital and mindshare, and providing job security for contributors) along with some new characteristics; The system is decentralised architecturally with ownership not being controlled by an elusive group of executives, the system is further decentralised politically with all executive power diverted to a balanced group of token holders determining what creative garments to fund and produce (Buterin, 2017). If someone further does not agree on governance they are free to fork their own DAO and start their own brand with a new form of governance.

WHY DIGITAL FASHION?

The state of the fashion industry today is bleak; clothing production has doubled since 2000 and people bought 60% more garments in 2014 than in 2000 with 85% percent of these garments ending up in landfills (McFall-Johnsen, 2019). Most of this wastage is being driven by fast-fashion, a term used to describe. The industry artificially fuelling this practise through manipulative concepts such as seasonal collections, Zara itself releasing 24 collections each year (Hayward, 2021). In total fast-fashion accounts for 20% of all industrial pollution and issues surrounding the traditional supply chain of fashion range from outright plagiarism to ubiquitous human rights abuses through the opaque lense of international supply chains.

About

Dali DAO is a service organisation. $DAL Tokens give members property rights to the DAO. This is a means of quantifying social and internet activity. The benefits include:

  • Encouraging collaboration and designers to succeed together.
  • Offering new earning opportunities to people around the world who otherwise would not.
  • Creating clear incentive alignment between users, employees and founders.
  • Encodings digital royalties for designs that are often playgirised by big brands.
  • Collective education and support provided for free.
